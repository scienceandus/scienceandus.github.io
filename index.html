--- title: Home layout: default subtitle: We're a group of high schoolers teaching peers about science communication and cross-disciplinary fields that support STEM. header_buttons: - name: Learn More url: /about - name: Join the Team url: /join primary: true section_one_image_url: '/img/first-event-students-laughing.jpg' section_one_headline: Making the complex compelling section_one_text: >

Net neutrality, environmental pollution, social media privacy—all those issues involve science, and affect much more than just scientists.

Understanding the fundamentals of STEM is crucial, yet many view it as intimidating or irrelevant. Science communication—explaining complex topics in engaging, clear ways—can bridge this gap.

Science & Us enables STEM-oriented students to better explain their passions to non-experts, and teaches them about interdisciplinary careers, like science media and policy, that are rarely mentioned in school.

section_one_buttons: - name: Learn More url: /about section_two_left_headline: Summer 2018 Workshop section_two_left_text: >

In June, we held an event at the Boston University Metcalf Science Center for 40 high school students. Invited guests from Harvard, MIT, Tufts, Boston University, and STAT taught the students about:

  • Using social media and podcasts for #scicomm
  • Making presentations, graphics, and explanations clear and interesting to different audiences
  • Day-to-day activities of a space librarian, science diplomat, and more!
section_two_left_buttons: - name: View Photos url: /summer-2018-photos section_two_right_headline: Winter 2018 Workshop section_two_right_text: >

This winter, we're introducing a new model that emphasizes giving students opportunities to create their own science communication pieces, learn by doing, and deepen their understanding of a topic of interest.

Before the event, accepted participants will draft the beginnings of their work, whether it's the first paragraph of an article, storyboard sketches of a video, or another medium. Our workshop will have sessions, led by practitioners and experts in various fields, to help students develop and ultimately share their work.
